Geology

  • Akin to forensic science
    • Discover the past through clues
  • Focus on the past
  • Focus in class is on the lithosphere (the rocks)
    • Volcanoes contribute interior material
      • Lots of water/gasses; sulfur indicates imminent eruptions
  • Why is it important?
    • Formation of landscapes
    • Natural resource exploration
    • Mitigating hazards
    • Understanding environmental change
    • Evolution of planet(s) and life
  • Science
    • Using scientific method to solve problems
      • Observe, hypothesis, test
    • Hypothesis
      • Should explain observations
      • Makes a prediction
      • Falsifiable - able to be proven wrong

Nebular Hypothesis

  • Gasses began to contract
    • Primary catalyst: gravity
    • Collisions cause condension and formation of larger objects
  • Earth gets really hot
    • Collisions
    • Compression
    • Radioactivity
  • The Moon
    • Formed by impact
    • Material from Earth ejected and condenses
    • Tilted the Earth's axis from impact

Chemical Differentiation

  • Process of Earth going from molten to solid
  • Heavy stuff goes to the core
  • Low density on the outside
  • Cooling forms the crust
  • Layers
    • Categorized by Elemental Differentiation
      • Crust, Mantle, Core
    • Categorized by Physical/Mechanical Properties
      • Lithosphere, asthenosphere, mesosphere, outer core, inner core
    • Crust and upper mantle form the lithosphere, 100 - 250km thick
    • Lower mantle forms the asthenosphere and mesosphere
    • Crust
      • Brittle like ice, solid
      • Oceanic: 3-15km thick, 3.0g/cm3 density
      • Continental: 20-70km thick, 2.7g/cm3 density
    • Mantle
      • Fluid, gradually goes to solid
      • 82% of Earth's volume, 3.3-11g/cm3 density

Rock Cycle

  • Geologic process to change rocks from one type to another
  • Principle of Uniformitarianism
    • States that processes now work the same as they always have
  • 3 Types: Igneous, Sedimentary, Metamorphic
  • Igneous Rock
    • Formed by cooling of magma. Extrusive if cooled at the surface, instrusive if cooled underground
  • Sedimentary Rock
    • Formed by sediments that have deposited, buried, cemented, and lithified
  • Metamorphic Rock
    • Formed by pre-existing rock that has been exposed to extreme pressure and heat to change

Plate Tectonics

  • What is it?
    • A model to explain Earth functions
    • Formally: A set of ideas explaining the observed motion of Earth's lithosphere through the mechanisms of subduction and seafloor spreading, which in turn, generate Earth's major features, including continents and ocean basins
    • The lithosphere can be broken into 'plates' and these interact in 3 main ways
  • Divergent Boundaries
    • Plates most away from one another
    • Rock gets younger the closer it is to the boundary
    • Elevation is high at the boundary and gradually lowers with distance to the boundary
    • Shallow (0km - 10km) depth earthquakes occur
    • Sparse volcanic activity, notable volcanoes at Iceland and other small islands
    • As plates move apart, the space between them is filled with molten rock
      • A rift valley forms, and tends to have lots of volcanoes and faults
      • Seafloor spreading
        • Rising magma cools into new oceanic crust as it fills the gap between plates
      • Increase in elevation is caused by hot, bouyant, and less dense magma forming the new crust
    • Convergent Boundaries
      • Plates collide into one another
      • 3 subtypes of boundary
        • Oceanic - Continental
        • Oceanic - Oceanic
        • Continental - Continental
      • Earthquakes range from shallow (0km - 10km) at the boundary to intermediate (10km - 30km) to deep (30km - 300km) in depth
      • Extensive mountain range of volcanoes (called a volcanic arc) and heavy volcanic activity
      • Younger, less-dense material subducts underneath older, more dense material
      • A low trench forms on the subducting side and high mountains on the other
    • Transform Boundaries
      • Plates slide next to one another
      • No age distinction between sides
      • Causes a perceived 'bending' of divergent boundaries
      • Causes no notable volcanic activity
      • Heavy shallow (0km - 10km) depth earthquakes
  • What drives plate motion?
    • Slab-pull: Strongest, occurs at convergent boundary from the weight of the subducting side being pulled down
    • Mantle-convection: Second strongest, caused by movement in the asthenosphere. Think of a boat on an ocean current; the plate is the boat and the ocean is the asthenosphere
    • Ridge-push: Weakest, caused at divergent boundaries from gravity pulling down on the newly formed crust

Matter and Minerals

  • Minerals
    • Definition: A naturally occurring inorganic solid
    • They have an ordered molecular structure
    • They also have a definite (set) chemical composition
    • Building blocks of rocks
  • Composition
    • Elements
      • Building blocks of minerals (and everything else)
      • 100+ known
    • Atomic Structure
      • Nucleus of protons and neutrons
      • Electrons live in the electron cloud
      • Outermost electrons are called valence electrons, and are responsible for forming bonds
      • Atomic Number: Number of Protons
      • Atomic Mass: Sum of Protons and Neutrons
      • Isotopes: Elements with the same atomic number but different atomic masses
    • Ions
      • Charged Atoms
      • Cations have a positive charge (less electrons)
      • Anions have a negative charge (more electrons)
      • Anions are larger because the pull from the protons is weaker on the electrons
    • Chemical Bonding
      • Formation of compounds by combining elements
      • Ionic Bonds
        • Atoms gain or lose valence electrons
        • Mineral example: Salt (Halite, NaCl)
      • Covalent Bonds
        • Sharing of one or more electrons
        • These are the strongest kind of bond
      • Metallic Bonds
        • Valence electrons are able to migrate between atoms
        • These are the weakest kind of bond
  • Structure of Minerals
    • Polymorphs
      • Minerals of the same composition (elements) but different crystalline structure
  • Physical Properties
    • Crystal Form
      • External expression of internal atomic arrangement
      • Determined by ionic size
    • Luster
      • Appearance in reflected light
      • Can be metallic (shiny) or non-metallic (dull)
    • Color
      • Unreliable in identifying minerals
      • Changes due to slight differences in chemistry
    • Streak
      • Color when turned to powder
    • Hardness
      • Resistance to scratching
      • Diamond is the hardest, talc is the weakest
    • Cleavage
      • Tendency to break from weak bonds
  • Fractures
    • A break not corresponding to chemical bonds
    • 3 kinds: conchoidal, fibrous, and irregular
  • Specific Gravity
    • Ratio of the mineral weight to water weight of the same volume
    • Basically a dimensionless measure of density
  • Elements in Continental Crust
    • Most common two are oxygen and silicon
  • Mineral Families
    • Silicates are the most common (example: Quartz)
    • Forms of silicates
      • Single Tetrahedron: Olivine
      • Single Chain: Pyroxene
      • Double Chain: Amphibole
      • Sheet Silicate: Mica
      • Three Dimesional Framework: Quartz

Igneous Rocks

  • Found at divergent plate boundaries and volcanoes
  • Two kinds: Extrusive (external) and Intrusive (internal)
    • Cools differently; intrusive cools slowly and extrusive cools rapidly
    • Hawaii - Extrusive, El Capitan - Intrusive
  • Geothermal Gradient
    • The change in temperature with depth
    • Melting beings where melting curve meets the geothermal gradient and intersects
  • Crystallization of Magma
    • Precipitation of mineral grains (crystals form and sink)
    • As magma cools, orderly crystalline structures form
  • Magma
    • Molten rock that contains crystals and gasses
      • Water Vapor
      • Carbon Dioxide
      • Sulfur Dioxide
    • Creating a magma
      • Heat a rock to its melting point
      • Lower the melting point by decreasing the pressure (raise elevation) or adding fluid (water)
    • Magma from Pressure Drop
      • Occurs at divergent plate boundaries
      • Called decompression melting
    • Magma from Adding Fluids
      • Occurs at convergent plate boundaries
    • Magma from Heat
      • Creates magma plumes that rise
    • Evolution of Magmas
      • Changing a magma's composition
        • Magmatic Differentiation: seperation of a melt from earlier formed crystals
        • Assimilation: magma incorporates surrounding rock
        • Magma Mixing: two chemicall different magmas mix together
  • Bowen's Reaction Series
    • Sequence of crystallization of a cooling silicate magma
      • Discontinuous Series Order: Olivine, Pyroxine, Amphibole, Mica
      • Continuous Series: Plagioclase feldspar
      • Amount of silicon increases as temperature lowers
      • More silicon makes the magma more viscous and less explosive
      • Iron, magnesium and calcium content lowers with temperature
  • Igneous Rock Classification
    • Composition (mineral components)
    • Texture (size, shape, crystal arrangement)
      • Determined by: rate of cooling, amount of silica, and amount of dissolved gas
      • Cooling
        • Slow (5°C/Million Years) - results in large crystals and few nucleation sites
        • Rapid (100°C/Million Years) - results in small crystals and many nucleation sites
      • Types
        • Aphanitic: fine-grained, crystals too small to see unaided
        • Phaneritic: coarse-grained, large crystals
        • Porphyritic: large crystals embedded in a fine-grained matrix
        • Glassy: lacks a crystalline structure because of rapid cooling
        • Vesicular: contains lots of cavities from trapped gas (porous)
  • Igneous Structures
    • Batholiths: cooled magma chambers
    • Sills: magma offshoots parallel to the rock layers
    • Dikes: magma offshoots perpendicular to the rock layers
    • Laccoliths: magma that has settled between two rock layers
    • Forms
      • Concordant (parallel - sills and laccoliths)
      • Discordant (not parallel - dikes and batholiths)
